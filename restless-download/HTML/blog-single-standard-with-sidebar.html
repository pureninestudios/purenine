5 Essential Design lessons from Design Masters

What makes a good design? Well it’s not only how it looks, but how it works and feels. A good design is clear and guides user to the most important parts of a website and encouraging them to take actions. So what we mean that design needs to be clear? Well, for start there are some standards in where you should put navigation, search, how the navigation should work, etc. Does this mean you can’t experiment? Off course it doesn’t, but when you experiment, do it wisely.

"Design is not only how it looks, but how it works and feels too."

I remember when we first started creating premium themes. We were new in this industry and always strove for perfect design. Design in premium theme was everything. But with time, we started exploring better usage of it. We thought, well it may be good to make a beautiful multipurpose theme, but what advantage will user who bought it have? Well, he will have amazing design, something to show off but we wanted to offer

something more. In that time, everything about beautiful designs was just it. The design was great, amazing images... but what about real content? What happens when user downs’t have a lot of content, what then, how will his site look? So we decided we need to go in a bit different direction. We started exploring real sites, real informations, niche sites, niche industries and wonder what one client would need? Step by step we turned to niche websites and themes. We started exploring what type of information should that site have. How will it look when there is just a few blocks of content, how it will work, how it will feel? We started to think about user experience on the first place. We started to explore types of navigations, animations, smooth scrolling, how we can provide the best experience when the user is on the site, even though there is just few sections on the page.

The first theme that was a great success was Elvyre. It was aimed for small companies from finance and IT industry, with shop option. We managed to make clear design with subtle animations, where every pixel had a purpose, and every element looked interesting and most important usable! We saw a lot of websites made with this theme, from very small companies to very large and we were pleased with what we see. The design was usable and looked and worked and felt great on both of these websites. Well, for us that means a good design. When you can tweak it and even though you don’t have amazing images and 15 sections on the page, it looks nice, professional and feels great to look at it.

So to wrap it up, designing a theme is quite different then work for clients, but the main goal is the same: creating a design that will look amazing, work fast, have unique feel, engage users and most important of it all, to have great user experience. A website that you will love to visit and spend time on it, weather to read the articles, looking at animations and how it works and feels, shop products or something else. That’s a great design and we’ll continue to work hard to bring only the best to you, our buyers.
