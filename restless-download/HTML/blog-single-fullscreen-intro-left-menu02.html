Learning UX fundamentals.
How to overcome typical UX fails.

  • by Jonathan Stephens
  • Design & UX
  • 245

A great design doesn’t mean much if the UI is not also perfect. In this article we’ll explore some UX fundamentals and how to overcome some typical UX fails.

Everyone is talking about UX these days, right? Everywhere where you go, any online blog you visit they all talk and write about great UX and how it is important for a website. And yes, they are right. A great design doesn’t mean so much, if the UX isn’t good, or better say perfect. So what makes a great UX and how to create great UX.

Well, in my humble opinion don’t start from Photoshop. If you do, that’s a first fail. Let’s say you are working for a client who has a transportation company. The company is pretty large, they offer a really wide range of services from land transportation to whole supply chain management. Your client needs a new website, because his current one looks old and outdated. So what’s the first step you should do? Don’t immediately start exploring great design ideas and some fancy looking websites. First ask your client if he has any analytics on his page. He probably does, or at least you’ll hope he does. Let’s say he doesn’t have any analytics, which makes the job even more complex. So first, go to his old website and place analytics on it. While you gather information about his company, collect materials for his new website, while you collect information about what problems are they now facing, probably one or two weeks will pass. You can also make an agreement that you’ll first study the current situation of his website for example a month if he can wait that long. The data you collect with analytics will tell you (although it’s a pretty short period), what pages ar e most visited, where the user lands first when he comes to a website, does he open one page, two page, how long he stays on that website and so on. Why are this informations important? Well, some designers jump immediately into creation of a brand new website and lose all the functionality and UX of an old one. This could be fail... Maybe old website has good UX, but design is a bit old and that part is a problem, not UX. So this part of information is vital. Check the analytics of existing site first. Now you overcame the first UX fail - “start designing immediately”.

Now that you have your conclusions you can start thinking about how can you make this site better. The nice way of doing it is persona approach. Make fictional characters, give them name, education status, etc. and think about how would they behave when they’ll visit your clients site. It’s a nice role play game that can help you in creating a good UX for targeted audience. Oh and yes, you need to determine a targeted audience. It’s funny when you ask a client what’s your targeted audience and they say all. Well, it can’t be right. Why? For starters a DJ for example who is in music business won’t need a large logistics company to handle a supply chain for him, right? Ok, maybe in some distant future he will, but that’s not quite possible. So next step is to determine which is your target audience and explore it’s culture and how they behave on a website. It’s not that hard, believe me. Why is that important? For example a chinese market is very different from European or American. Their websites are “really busy”, not clean and clear as most of us are used to. Also, if you are targeting multiple markets you will probably have multilingual site. So it’s important to know this in the first place, because one word in English for example has 5 letters, while in German, that same word has 20 letters. So be aware of that as it will influence your UX a lot. So, you have determined your targeted audience, good for you, another UX fail overcome - “design for everyone”.

You can’t design for “EVERYONE”. Determine your targeted audience, explore their behavior on a website, their culture and then start from there.

So that’s it for this article. Hope you enjoyed yourself while reading this. We didn’t want to use lorem lpsum or anything similar as it’s a bit dull and boring. We wanted to engage you a bit, show you how we think about some subjects and hopefully teach you something new.
